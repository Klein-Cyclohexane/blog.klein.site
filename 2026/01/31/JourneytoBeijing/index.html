Journey to Beijing

After a four-month separation, I took the train to Beijing again.

Peking University at the end of January is far less crowded than it is during the National Day holiday, with only senior undergraduate students, postgraduates and a few tourists moving around the campus.

The sunshine is bright, and the gnarled branches of the trees lining the road stretch into unique arcs against the blue sky—a sight rarely seen in the south.

Our two lazybones didn't go anywhere to have fun. We slept in till half past eleven every day, got up for lunch, and then wandered around the campus. There were plenty of people skating on the ice rink of Weiming Lake.

February 1st coincided with the 14th day of the lunar month, and the moon was round and bright.
